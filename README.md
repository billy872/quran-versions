# The Different Versions of the Quran

**Live Site:** https://billy872.github.io/quran-versions/

## Overview

A comprehensive, evidence-based investigation into the 10+ canonical Quranic recitations (qira'at), their historical origins, documented textual differences, and manuscript evidence.

## Key Findings

- **10 canonically accepted Quranic recitations** exist with 20 distinct transmissions
- **1,354 documented variants** between Hafs and Warsh alone
- **10,000+ total documented variants** across all readings (Islamic scholarship)
- Differences include: different words, verb forms, singular/plural nouns, pronouns, grammatical structures

## The 10 Canonical Readings

1. **Hafs 'an 'Asim** (Most popular - 95% of Muslims)
2. **Warsh 'an Nafi'** (North/West Africa - 3-5%)
3. **Qalun 'an Nafi'** (Libya, Tunisia)
4. **Al-Duri 'an Abu 'Amr** (Sudan, West Africa)
5. **Hisham 'an Ibn Amir** (Yemen)
6. Al-Bazzi, Qunbul, Al-Susi, Ibn Dhakwan, and others

## Historical Timeline

- **610-632 CE:** Quranic revelation period — oral transmission begins
- **~650 CE:** Caliph Uthman standardizes consonantal text
- **8th Century:** Famous readers develop distinct traditions
- **936 CE:** Ibn Mujahid canonizes 7 readings
- **1429 CE:** Ibn al-Jazari adds 3 more (total: 10)
- **1924:** Egyptian Cairo Edition makes Hafs dominant via mass printing
